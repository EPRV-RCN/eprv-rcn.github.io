RCN Community Day Following EPRV 6

Event Details

Dates: Friday, July 4th, 2025

Location: Porto, Portugal

Venue: Centro de astrofisica da Univerisdade do Porto; Rua das Estrelas, 4150-762 Porto, Portugal

Description

The RCN will host a collaboration and networking day on Friday, July 4th, following EPRV 6 in Porto, Portugal.

Takeaways

Around 30 particpants joined, from all career stages, and discussed a variety of EPRV-related topics, including but not limited to:

  • What are the biggest challenges facing the field?
  • What actions need to be taken in the next 5-10 years in the EPRV community?
  • What community resouces should the RCN proivde?
  • ?

Takeaways regarding big challenges include:

  • Tackling supergranulation.
  • Coordinating PLATO follow-up.

Takeaways regarding EPRV community needs include:

  • A unified, yet multidimensional approach to planet hunting.
  • Improved understanding between instrument behaviours.
  • Improved understanding of the instrument and stellar impact at the spectral level.
  • Improved scheduling between telescopes and more coordinated (potentially multi-wavelength) campaigns.
  • Improved simulations of stellar variability, including but not limited to, high resolution 3D MHD spectral synthsis of spots, plage/faculae, and quiet photosphere for FGKM dwarf stars.

Takwaways regarding desired community resources:

  • Networking, collaboration and fruitful exchanges.
  • Tutorials on how to access data and/or open-source community tools for data reduciton and analysis.
  • Overview lists and links to relevant data repositories, software, facilities, review papers, recorded talks etc.
  • Guidence on career oppertunities and open positions.
  • Helpful visualisations for including in presentations.

Resultant RCN action items:

  • The RCN will continue to provide community workshops and collaboration events (next workshokshop, in December 2025, will be on the interface between solar facilities/data and the EPRV community).
  • The RCN website will provide a repository where the community can share helpful visualisations (coming soon).
  • The RCN will develop an `Exoplanet Departments Tracking Sheet' to track institutes with active EPRV staff.
  • The RCN website will provide a starting point overview of current EPRV related software (spear-headed by Federica Rescigno).
  • The RCN website will provide a starting point overview of data archives and repositoties (spear-headed by Sarah Lodgson).
